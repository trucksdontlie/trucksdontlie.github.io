Section1

A Call for Change operates on several levels. On the one hand, change. We seek to inform and illuminate on a problem that has a certain urgency, but is not yet defined. If we can think of alternative solutions for automation and labor we can revert what has typically remained as a convoluted relationship. We truly do believe that the technology at hand has inherent possibilities to have a positive effect on the labor that it could, at some point in time, replace. The specificity of this issue also allows for a fraternal understanding between the different Unions across the planet, that can help to develop this type of ideas. (maybe analogy of marx’s wokers of the world unite)

Section2

On the other hand, a Call. This problem has several layers of complexity, with several overlays of different disciplinary backgrounds. We would like to make a call for papers in the realm of where this problems stands: Unions, Automation and Logistics. We believe that by having people from very different backgrounds speak about the same problem from divergent angles could produce a good body of information to later on continue this research. That’s why we are looking for an interdisciplinary journal that would allow us to edit articles, from machine vision and highly technical information, historical struggle of automation and unions, how could union's change, to the logics of logistics and macro economics. We hope this body of research could help other be informed about the complexities and potentialities that lie in overlapping what in the past has been oppositional.
